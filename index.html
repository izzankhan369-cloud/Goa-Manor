Heritage & Legacy

A Colonial Relic, Reborn in Splendour

Nestled within the basilica-studded hills of Old Goa, The Manor is one of the last standing Portuguese colonial estates on the Konkan coast. Built in 1642 for a Portuguese nobleman, its laterite stone walls, carved teak doorways, and frescoed ceilings have been painstakingly restored — not renovated — preserving every crack and chronicle of four centuries of Goan history.

Today, The Manor accommodates just six suites, each named for a chapter of its storied past. Guests arrive not as tourists but as inheritors — of silence, of candlelit suppers, of mornings that belong only to the monsoon-damp garden and the distant bells of Sé Cathedral.
